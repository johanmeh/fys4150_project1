\section{Project 1 a)}

We are attempting to solve the equation: 

\begin{align*}
	-u''(x) & = f(x), x \in (0, 1), u(0) = u(1) = 0
\end{align*}

which can be approximated as:

\begin{equation}
	v_i" \approx -\frac{v_{i-1}- 2v_i + v_{i+1}}{h^2}
\end{equation}

Assumed $n= 4$, it can be shown that this can be represented as a Toepliz-matrix by setting: 

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
	v
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
	 v_1 & v_2 & v_3 & v_4
\end{bmatrix}
\]

For which 

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
	v_1" \\  v_2" \\ v_3" \\ v_4"
\end{bmatrix}
	\approx -\frac{1}{h^2}
\begin{bmatrix}
	-(v_{0} -2v_1 +v_2) \\
	-(v_1 -2v_2 +v_3) \\
	-(v_{2} -2v_3 +v_4) \\
	-(v_{3} -2v_4 +v_5) 
\end{bmatrix}
\]

The boundary conditions are set to $v_0 = v_{n+1} = 0$, so the expression becomes: 

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
	2 & -1 & 0 & 0 \\
	-1 & 2 & -1 & 0 \\
	0 & -1 & 2 & -1 \\
	0 & 0 & -1 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
	v_1 \\  v_2 \\ v_3 \\ v_4
\end{bmatrix}
	= -h^2
\begin{bmatrix}
	f_1 \\
	f_2 \\
	f_3 \\
	f_4
\end{bmatrix}
\]

In which $f_i$ is known.
